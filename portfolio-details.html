Identifying Low-Profit Products & Strategies to Improve Performance

Product Profit

Key Analysis Points:
  • Marketing Suite product has the lowest profitability.
  • Recorded a financial loss of $3,473.
  • Potential reasons: ineffective marketing, poor discount placement, low market interest.

Based on the analysis of the graph, the Marketing Suite product has the lowest profitability, with a loss of $3,473. This indicates that not only is the product failing to generate profit, but it is also creating a financial burden for the company. These losses may be due to several factors, such as ineffective marketing strategies, poorly optimized discount placements, or a lack of market interest in the product.

Trend Marketing Suite Profit

Key Analysis Points:
  • Mostly unprofitable (Jan 2020 – Dec 2023)
  • Peak profit: $747 (Nov 2021)

Looking at the profit trends from January 2020 to December 2023, the Marketing Suite product consistently remained in the negative zone. It only achieved positive profits on a few occasions, with its highest recorded profit in November 2021 at $747. However, this is still significantly lower compared to other products. For instance, the best-performing product in the dataset, Alchemy, reached its peak profit in October 2022 with $10,992, highlighting a major gap in profitability.

Further Analysis

To understand why the Marketing Suite product has such low profitability, we will conduct a deeper analysis by examining the relationship between discounts, sales, and profits. Additionally, we will assess market interest in different countries.

Impact of Discounts on Profit and Sales

Key questions to consider:

  • Are the discounts effective in increasing sales?
  • Are the discounts too high, leading to lower profit margins and losses?
Key Analysis Points:
  • Discounts Did Not Boost Sales or Profit
  • Ineffective Discount Strategy
  • Heavy Discounts Shrink Profit Margins

From the graph analysis, it is evident that offering discounts did not significantly boost sales or profits. In fact, the data suggests that higher discounts often resulted in lower sales and profits. This trend can be observed in the graph, where an increase in discount rates correlates with a decline in both sales and profit. On the other hand, when discounts were smaller or not applied at all, profit and sales remained at a relatively higher level. This suggests that the discounting strategy for this product has been ineffective and has failed to drive meaningful growth. One of the main reasons behind this trend is the shrinking profit margin due to heavy discounting. When large discounts are applied, selling prices drop, reducing profit per unit. If sales volume does not increase significantly to compensate for this lower margin, the total profit remains low—or even negative.

Market Interest Analysis by Location

Profit Performance by Country The countries were classified into three clusters based on average profit:

  • Blue = Profit above $100
  • Light Blue = Profit between $0-$100
  • Light Gray = Negative profit
Key Analysis Points:
  • Japan: High Sales (14,493) but Negative Profit (-30% Discount)
  • U.S.: Highest Sales, 15% Discount, Small Positive Profit ($3.40/Transaction)
  • Egypt: Highest Total Profit ($1,013) with No Discounts (23% Margin)
  • Saudi Arabia: Highest Profit Margin (27.5%), Strong Revenue Efficiency

For example, despite high sales volume in Japan (14,493 units sold), the average profit per transaction was negative due to a 30% discount. While this discount strategy did increase sales, it ultimately caused major financial losses. In contrast, in the United States, which had the highest sales volume overall, the average discount was only 15%, allowing the company to maintain a small but positive profit of $3.40 per transaction. The highest total profit was recorded in Egypt, with an average profit of $1,013, where no discounts were applied. Although Egypt’s profit margin was 23%, the product remained efficient in generating revenue. Meanwhile, Saudi Arabia had the highest profit margin at 27.5%. While the total sales and profit were lower than in Egypt, the high profit margin indicates strong efficiency in generating revenue.

Recommended Strategies to Improve Marketing Suite’s Performance

  1. Strategically apply discounts in high-potential countries like Egypt and Saudi Arabia, ensuring that discounts boost sales without harming profit margins.
  2. Reduce discounts in high-sales countries and monitor sales performance over time to assess the impact.
  3. Discontinue sales in low-performing countries where high discounts fail to drive sufficient sales, such as Russia, which had only 1,584 sales despite an 80% discount.
  4. Focus on high-value and repeat customers, such as:
    • AmersourceBergen (638 units, 9 repeat purchases)
    • Amazon (1,044 units, 4 repeat purchases)
    These customers can drive long-term revenue without requiring excessive discounts.
  5. Explore alternative marketing strategies, such as:
    • Bundling products with higher-value offerings
    • Loyalty programs to encourage repeat purchases
    • Targeted digital advertising for better audience engagement

By implementing these more effective strategies, the company can increase profitability without over-relying on discounts, which, as seen in the data, often lead to financial losses.
